On your Interview Q&A:

Write down 10 to 20 line answers to the most common questions (each should take min 30 seconds max 2 minutes to read):

 

Your answers take 30 seconds to 2 minutes to read?

Tell me about yourself This corresponds to an expanded version of the second paragraph of your Cover Letter

What interests you about this opening?/why do you want to work for us? Highlights of your skills. Talk about your

What do you know about our company? You did the research on step 5 for the cover letter.

Why did you leave your last job?/Why are you thinking about leaving your current job? You've outgrown your job, you want to expand to other skills, this job has a skillset that interests you.

 

Are your answer specific and detailed?

Tell me about your experience at (past job) Always from a positive standpoint.

What experience do you have doing (Keyword they asked for on the offer)? 2 minute answer. They will ask for more if they want. If they ask you a question you don't know, here's a template:

"This particular subject is something that I am not fully familiarized with, however it is one of the things that attracted my attention from your offer: I am looking forward to work in an environment with room to learn and develop skills that will suit you and the company".

 

Are there examples of your skills/experiences in your answers?

Tell me about your biggest strengths Relate them to the job.

 

Is your "biggest strength" answer solid, specific and valuable?

Tell me about your weaknesses The most important things are to have specific examples, give a positive spin and how you're working on it. If your biggest weakness is public speaking, you could say something like this:

I've had lots of difficulties when speaking in front of a crowd. I've taken leadership roles on some volunteering work and signed up for theatre workshops, and I know there is a long way to go but I am working on it and I feel like I am making progress.

If your biggest weakness is your lack of patience, you could phrase it this way:

Whenever there is a deadline approaching, if I feel like someone's not going to make their contribution on time, I've had a tendency to take over and finish the work myself. I've identified this lack of patience and I'm working on raising signals and coaching if I can before taking over, to respect my coworkers and make sure we all understand each other as well as the goal at hand.
 

On your "weakness question", did you explain how you're working to solve it?

Did you use your answers from the requirements document here?

Tell me about when you had to take initiative … you had to deal with a difficult customer … you had to respond to a crisis … you had to give difficult feedback to an employee Remember the STAR method: Situation, Task, Action, Result.

 

Did you follow the STAR (Situation, Task, Action, Result) method on the answers that require it?

How does this position fit in with the career path you envision for yourself? Always from a positive perspective.

Where do you see yourself in five years? You should still be in the company!

What salary are you expecting to make here? Do your research. Glassdoor and Salary.com will help you find out how much you should get payed. Ask for the high range. If you have to negotiate, Here's a brilliant video that perfectly explains how to do it.

This script will make it easier to improvise/adapt better according to the context of the question. Have your friends ask you these questions over and over, and play devil's advocate.
