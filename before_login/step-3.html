7 steps to the job you want

Step 3

MATCH YOUR ONLINE PROFILE TO THE OPENIN

Recruiters will look you up online. Prepare for it.

 
 

in

FIRST: LinkedIn
 

 

Check out my profile for an example.

 

You must have a LinkedIn profile that has:

 

A clear picture showing your face.

A Headline right below your name: this is a five word phrase that reflects the opening, not what you've done. If you've done accounting but want to be a meth dealer, your headline will be "Walter White is a Meth Dealer".

A Summary of five to ten lines describing what you want to do. Keywords of the job you want must be there.

Here's an example you can adapt:

CDN Architect looking for opportunities to take my skills and techniques to the next level. Over 6 years of hard work and persistence have allowed me to enjoy working on big projects and fully understand how to lead and perform on stresful conditions. I seek to expand my expertise to advance my career and prove to you that I will be a valuable asset to your company.

Specialties: CDN, Project Management, Streaming, QA, Testing.

Be sure to add the "Specialties" (and an extra "i" if you're in the UK) line at the end of the summary like in the example above.

Add your work experiences and copy and paste your answers to the requirements on Step 2 to the corresponding job.

Add your degrees and copy and paste your answers to the requirements on Step 2 to the corresponding degree.

Copy and paste the skills you wrote about in Step 2.

Get at least 50 connections. Import your email contacts if needed.

Add your degrees and copy and paste your answers to the requirements on Step 2 to the corresponding degree.

Create a friendly public LinkedIn link. You will add it to your resume/cv later.

Add any projects, honors, awards you have.

 

SECOND: Google
 

 

Google your name, with/without city, experience on Google. "Anto Montani" (with quotes), Anto Montani, Anto Montani Paris, Anto Montani CDN . See what comes up on the first two Google pages. If you found it, they'll find it. Take care of it.

 

THIRD: Brand Yourself
 

 
My brandyourself profile.

Open a free account with brandyourself.com profile. They will help you improve your online profile:

 

Import your LinkedIn profile to make a brandyourself profile.

Rank the links they show you whether they're positive, negative or unrelated.

Submit links about you that are positve, so they can promote them.

 

FOURTH: Social Media
 

 

Clean up your Twitter account. Delete all tweets that might not help you make a good impression.

Make your Facebook profile as private as possible. If I'm not your friend, I should only be able to see your name, photo and that's it. Look at My profile.

Tumblr, Pinterest, Google+ (seriously?), everything that shares personal info about you should be locked down. It helps to know you use social media, it DOESN'T help to share your personal details with everybody. Unless you want to work in the industry, of course.

Next time you go through this step, just add the new answers from the new job opening to your LinkedIn profile work experience/degree/skills. Done. On to Step 4
